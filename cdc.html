What Is the Purpose of the Cyber Defense Club?

The Cyber Defense Club (CDC) is a student-run club dedicated to fostering an innovative, fast-paced environment that promotes learning and shared knowledge of cybersecurity and related fields.

The Cyber Defense Club is a place where students who are interested in cybersecurity can attend and learn about the field, participate in games/challenges, and make professional connections. The CDC is a student run club designed to advocate for an innovative, fast-paced environment that promotes learning, and a shared knowledge of cybersecurity and the fields corresponding with it. In addition to promoting these ideals the club provides members with the opportunity to get involved with and exposed to the field, all while improving their cyber awareness and expertise. Members of the Cyber Defense Club attend cyber related events, conferences and competitions; and even host an event of their own where teams compete against each other to test their skills and abilities within a controlled environment.

Source: CDC Presence Page

Who Oversees the Club?

The faculty advisors provide guidance, but the club is directed and managed through the student-led officers. In conjunction with the advisors and other department faculty, they plan and execute cyber related events, meetings, and competitions.

Who Can Participate?

The CDC is open to all students, not just cybersecurity majors or minors. Anyone interested in learning about cybersecurity and improving their personal, family, or organizational cyber hygiene is welcome to join!

How to Join?

Students can join events by monitoring CDC announcements on social media (Instagram: msmu_cyber) or through emails and announcments by professors. Attending a meeting or event is the best way to get started, no sign up required!

For cybersecurity majors completing a capstone, participation in an NCL (National Cyber League) competition is required.

What Happens at a CDC Related Event?

Events and meetings often include:

  • Cyber-related games (e.g., Kahoots).
  • Group trips to major cybersecurity conferences. For example, the CDC took a group of female cyber students to the Technica UMD Conference/Hackathon directed towards women in STEM (one of the largest) in 2024.
  • Workshops led by industry professionals on cybersecurity tools and techniques.
  • Participation in national and regional competitions, including the National Cyber League and CCSC-E.
  • Guest speakers discussing cybersecurity careers, certifications, resume writing, and professional networking.
  • Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions to test and improve cyber skills. The CDC has also sponsored teams to travel for regional cyber competitions.
  • In conjunction with the capstone, but not a requirement, the CDC sponsors one or more teams to compete in the National Cyber League's regular competitions.
  • Inviting guest speakers to discuss topics such as certifications, work life, interviews, resume writing, and other career-related opportunities.

What Else to Know before Considering Particiption?

As noted, specialized knowledge is not required to join or participate in any of the activities, including the NCL competitions. The club's activities and the skills to which the members are exposed will improve a member's personal cyber hygiene, making them safer when engaging with the online world. It is a club whose mission is very applicable in today's world, considering the frequency of attacks and the damage they do to business' when compromised. Furthermore, being a student-led organization means that the timing of events, meetings, and general activities is amenable to academic commitments.
